Omurice

Omelette rice, or omurice, is a popular Japanese dish that combines fried rice with a tender, flavorful omelette.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked and cooled white rice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup diced mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, peas, corn, or bell peppers)
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked protein (e.g., chicken, ham, or shrimp)
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup, plus extra for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons milk

Steps

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the mixed vegetables and cooked protein to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the protein is heated through.
  3. Stir in the cooked rice, breaking up any clumps with a spatula, and cook until heated through. Add the ketchup, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix everything together until the rice is evenly coated with ketchup. Remove the fried rice from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. In the same skillet, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-low heat. Pour in half of the egg mixture and cook until the edges are set but the center is still slightly runny, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Spoon half of the fried rice mixture onto one half of the omelette in the skillet. Gently fold the other half of the omelette over the rice, and carefully slide the omelette rice onto a plate. Repeat the process with the remaining egg mixture and fried rice to create a second serving.
  7. Drizzle the top of each omelette with additional ketchup, if desired, and serve immediately.
